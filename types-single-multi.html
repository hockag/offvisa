Single-entry and multi-entry visa

Under certain conditions, you will even be exempted from a visa

When it comes to visas, there are distinct types that offer different levels of entry and convenience. Here's a breakdown of single-entry and multi-entry visas:

Single-Entry Visa

A single-entry visa is designed for a solitary entry into a country within a specified timeframe. After departing from the country, you must initiate a new visa application should you intend to return. The single-entry visa is often the most economical and expedient option to acquire.

Multi-Entry Visa

Contrasting the single-entry visa, a multi-entry visa enables multiple arrivals and departures from the destination within its validity period, without necessitating repeated visa applications. However, please note that each visit might be subject to specific limitations on the number of days allowed. It's also important to verify the specific entry restrictions before initiating the application process.
